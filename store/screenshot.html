The Future of Renewable Energy

By Sarah Chen — 12 min read

The global energy landscape is undergoing a fundamental transformation as renewable technologies reach cost parity with fossil fuels across most major markets. This shift, driven by decades of research and falling manufacturing costs, is reshaping how nations plan their energy infrastructure.

Solar photovoltaic costs have declined by 89% since 2010, making it the cheapest source of new electricity generation in most of the world. The International Energy Agency projects that solar will account for the largest share of new power capacity additions through 2030.

Wind energy has seen similar cost reductions, particularly in offshore installations where turbine sizes have grown dramatically. The latest generation of offshore turbines can power over 16,000 homes each, a fourfold increase from designs just a decade ago.

Battery storage technology is the critical enabler. Without cost-effective storage, intermittent renewables cannot provide the reliable baseload power that modern economies require. Lithium-ion battery costs have fallen 97% since 1991.
